Garden of Love – Think-Aloud Observational Notes

Participant 1 (M, 21)
- Said “Wow, the grass feels huge, it’s like I’m tiny!” at start.
- Hesitated when approaching the ant line, unsure if touching would cause damage.
- Tried to pick up a random pebble before realizing the stone interaction popup.
- Said “Ah okay, I have to hit the red ant” after reading the hint.
- Completed the level in ~2 minutes; noted it was short but clear.
- Suggested “Make it clearer which stones are pickable.”

Participant 2 (F, 23)
- Quickly understood movement and camera controls.
- Verbalized: “I think I’m supposed to throw something at the red ant.”
- Missed the rock location initially, wandered around for ~30 seconds.
- Commented “The sound of ants is creepy but cool.”
- Suggested making the rock more visible.

Participant 3 (M, 25)
- Expressed confusion: “Am I allowed to walk into the sand area?”
- Got stuck trying to climb grass edges — said “I think I went outside the map?”
- Noticed the pop-up message but didn’t see the stone right away.
- Completed the task eventually; said “Okay, that was satisfying when the ants disappeared.”
- Suggested better boundary guidance or invisible walls.

Participant 4 (M, 20)
- Moved confidently but said “Controls feel a bit floaty.”
- Walked into ants multiple times before realizing it caused no immediate damage.
- Understood red ant target instantly after message appeared.
- Suggested adding a short animation or effect when throwing stone hits.

Participant 5 (F, 22)
- Commented on visuals: “The lighting looks really good — feels warm.”
- Missed stone pickup entirely; stood idle for ~15 seconds near ants.
- Asked, “Is there another way to cross?”
- Eventually found stone and said, “Ah, got it!”
- Suggested highlighting interactive items.

General Observations:
- All players enjoyed the small-world feeling and visuals.
- 3/5 players struggled to locate the rock.
- 4/5 players were unsure of map boundaries initially.
- Red ant mechanic was well understood once discovered.
- Average completion time: ~2–3 minutes per participant.
